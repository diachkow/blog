Building a Lock-Free Queue in Rust

date: 2026-02-18 | tags: [rust, concurrency, systems] | read_time: 12min

Lock-free data structures are the dark art of concurrent programming. They promise high throughput without the overhead of mutex contention, but they demand a deep understanding of memory ordering, compare-and-swap operations, and the ABA problem. In this post, we build a Michael-Scott queue from scratch in Rust, leveraging the type system to make our unsafe code as safe as possible.
